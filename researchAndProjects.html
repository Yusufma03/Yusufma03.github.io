Time Series Analyzing in Crowd Sensing

Supervised By Prof. Fan Wu

Since Dec. 2014, I was honored to join the Advanced Network Laboratory, supervised by Prof. Fan Wu. In most of the time, I was co-supervised by a very talented and nice Ph.D candidate Zhenzhe Zheng.
Our intuition is to find a way to analyze the stochastic and untrustworthy reports generated from the crowd sensing network. We noticed that in a crowd sensing network, the quality of the data will diverge to a large extent according to the different quality of the different devices, and one value at the time point will be synthesized by many reports at different places and different time. Thus, the methods used in traditional sensor networks no longer work in the crowd sensing network, and there is no such a work proposing a way to model the time series for the crowd sensing network. We hope such a model to have the following properties:

  • Effectiveness: It should be able to capture the correct trend of the time series even if there are noise and human intervention and give a good result.
  • Efficiency: The computation cost of the model should be controled under an acceptable bound. Ideally, it should converge in polynomial time.
  • Trustworthiness: It should be give a reliable interpretation for the trustworthiness of the users.

In our work, we proposed a trust-based Dynamic Bayesian Network to model the time series generated from the crowd sensing network, integrating the hidden variables, the time tick values, and the original reports, capturing the trustworthiness in the crowd sensing of the user. Meanwhile, we also proposed a trust-based EM algorithm to learn the model effectively, solving the problems above.
This could be my first paper as a first author, and we prepare to submit it to IEEE International Conference on Communications 2017.

Hot Topic Prediction in Social Networks

Supervised By Prof. Xiaofeng Gao

I was honored to join the Big Data Management and Application on Distributed Storage System research team supervised by Prof. Xiaofeng Gao, led by Renxuan Wang, a talented undergraduate since Nov. 2015. Our team was qualified as one of the members of the National Undergraduate Training Programs for Innovation and Entrepreneurship. In this program, I'm focusing on designing a brand new hot topic prediction method for the social networks.
Our intuition is that the social networks have strenched across people's daily life. The hot topic can reflect the important event of mass interest, hence predicting the hot topic can have many important practical value. However, we noticed that the definition of the hot topic is not so accurate, and the state-of-art prediction methods for topic are not powerful and effective enough. We aim to find an better approach to define what can be defined as a hot topic, and build a near real-time hot topic prediction system. Currently this work is still in progress.

AAAS: A Medical Diagnosis System Based on Machine Learning

Supervised By Prof. Bin Sheng

It was an honor to join Prof. Bin Sheng's Visual Media and Data Management Laboratory since Nov, 2015. In this project, I led a group of 3 people, designed and developed a automatic segmentation system for the abdominal adipose tissue, providing a better indicator for obesity-related diseases than BMI. The input is a DICOM raw image, and the output is the segmentation result, including the segmentation by Deep Neural Networks shown by black and white with error rate less than 2%, the rate of visceral adipose tissue over the subcutaneous adipose tissue, and a Volume Rendering of a series of contiguous slices implemented by OpenGL on MFC. Moreover, we use the CUDA GPU programming to accelerate the process of the segmentation, obtaining over 57% speed up.
